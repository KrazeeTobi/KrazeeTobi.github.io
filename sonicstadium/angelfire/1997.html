1997
The year of the Saturn!

<<1991 | 1992 | 1993 | 1994 | 1995 | 1996 | 1997 | 1998 | 1999 | 2000 | 2001>>

The Saturn was released in 1995/1996 and after a strong amount of Sega games released on it, Sega thought it was time for Sonic to move on from the Mega Drive onto their new 32-Bit console. Ironically, although this year was the greatest for Saturn/Sonic lovers, there was no real Sonic platforming sequel released, which was a shame, and also odd, as Sega and Sony were pretty much on Even Stevens at this point. Before Sony went and screwed up videogaming as we knew it:

Sonic Jam - Sega Saturn
May, 1997

This was really a compilation to ease the pain of many who had switched their Mega Drives for a Saturn and had missed those brilliant Sonic games of old. This compilation was from Sega though, and therefore was not your ordinary run of the mill compilations. Aswell as Sonic 1, 2, 3 and Knuckles but it also had a special 3D level called Sonic World, where you could see the hilarious Sonic movies, character Bios etc.

See more information on Sonic Jam - Saturn

Sonic R - Sega Saturn/Sega PC
November, 1997

Sonic R was a very special game to Saturn owners around the globe. This was the originality of Sega and Sonic Team that they had waited for, and in no means did they disappoint. If you MUST slag it off, there was no real challenge and once you had done everything, you may feel a bit unfilfilled. BUT, on the up side, this really was stated as the Best Saturn Game, and I have no doubts about that. Graphics were gorgeous, Sonic characters galore, many different paths through tracks (which is rare in racing games) and hidden items to collect while racing.
