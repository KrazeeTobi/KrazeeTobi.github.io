*Playtest*

<Well, I must say, I've seen some pretty impressive Fan Games, but Graphically speaking, this game looks set to blast them all outta the ocean!

Sonic Robo Blast 2, or more commonly known as SRB2, has been one of the most anticipated Fan Games to date. Do you know why? Sonic Team Junior intend to break Fan Game history by creating the first ever 3D Fan Game, and guess what? SFGC gets to preview it! Cool or what!?

For the first time ever, Eggman actually stops to plan his attack to Sonic more carefully, being beaten in the original SRB, which was a brilliant game in its own right, although won't break the Fan barrier like its sequel soon will. Robotnik only had his small army of badniks and his 'Doomship', a battleship he built. He found out about a Black Rock floating in space, and he decides to use the Black Rock as the perfect battle base. Eggman sets up on the Black Rock and starts his attack on the planet. Diverting Sonic, he destroys the Greenflower City Zone and continues his current world domination bender. Sonic is there to stop him, now with the assistance of Tails and Knuckles, to stop the rotten egg!

This project started off way back in March 1998, so no wonder everyone's getting excited about it. Based off of the Doom engine (that's why some of the screenshots have the Doom bar at the bottom), at the start of production, when the team were ready they moved off the engine, and test plays certainly prove the amount of time they've spent on this game. More than 3 years after production started, Dreadknux gets hold of a demo of the game, from Sonic Team Junior, no less. So, naturally, I put off everything and play the demo to pieces.

And what do I come across right at the very start? A title screen that looks a spiffing image of one from the Junior's bigger Brother, Sonic Team. So, they get a good title screen, big deal. Shut up. I'm getting to the more exciting bits... ^_^. I press a key, and a menu appears, Sonic 2 stylee, with a plethora of options to access when the game is finished. Granted, most of them weren't working at present, it is a demo after all. Upon many things selections ranged from control options to Enemy respawn counts. Quite handy if you're up for a challenge.

In the demo you get to play as Sonic, in the First level, Greenflower City Zone. Despite you only being able to play as Sonic, Tails is lost somewhere, in the actual code of the game. All the attributes of Tails is in the demo, you can't play as him because at present not all the Tails sprites are complete. The game also may not play the music on your PC, but STJr are fixing that little niggle as we speak.

Well, what else can I say, but Woah. Woah in the good way of course. Amazing 3D Graphics aside, the sprites are fantastic, and that's one hell of a compliment considering that all the sprites seem hand-drawn on PC. Don't underestimate this game for speed either. I almost did, thinking that due to the 3D graphics and being a demo Fan Game, I wasn't getting my hopes up about the speed of the game. What a shock that gave me then eh? Sonic's top speed will more often than not melt your eyes into your sockets.

The reactions to the button presses are quick and there's no delay either. Authentic Sonic sound effects are the order of the day here also as you hear the classic ring sound when you collect a bunch of the things. Everything just seems to be perfect, even in this demo, soundtrack apart.

One minor thing may be, as I was playing against Robotnik, he was incredibly hard to hit, and although there is a presence of a double jump, it is simply a method of gaining speed. This kind of double jump isn't bad in any case, I just think that the player could benefit from an actual homing attack. Aswell as this, and the only danger in playing this game, is that if you're the queasy type, do not play this game with your face near enough to the monitor. Seriously, it's not pleasant watching the speed of the game that close.

So what's to come then from the soon to be highly acclaimed (even more) Sonic Team Junior? Well, prepare yourself for another 8 Zones, not including Greenflower City, I am told some extra characters will be playable, should you do something to gain them first. There's even a prospect of creating your own levels, characters, music and if you're already quipped with some programming knowledge, an entire new game! This aspect will make the game a sure fire hit for definite! There will be network play, and even online play, with a supposedly 'cheat proof' time attack system. A grading system like Sonic Adventure 2 will be implemented too, and special goodies will be granted for reaching certain goals, plus there will be different routes throughout levels.

Well, I'm sure you'll all agree that Sonic Team Junior had their work cut out for them, and even after three years, they've delivered and made everyone that was in the dark before, very happy indeed. Rumours of this game being canned has certainly gone down the drain, as STJr definitely has the bottle to take on a massive job like this, and their game will no doubt be a success. With a step in Fan Gaming this big, I think the Team name will go down in Fan Game history, being just as popular in the Fan Game world as their Official counterpart, Sonic Team, already is in the Sega world. May Father and Junior walk hand in hand to success...
