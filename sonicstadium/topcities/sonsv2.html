*Playtest*

This game first reared its head during the recent (recent!? Six months ago? o_O) SAGExpo, Diablo was working on it, and the screenshot above which showcased it looked mighty impressive. Yum.

The demo has been released, and I must say, it plays very well. In the finished game, you can play as one of four hedgehogs - Diablo, Blaze, Hez Hedgehog (looks uncannily like H Hog?), and regular old Sonic. In the demo you could only play as Sonic. All four characters have different jump and speed attributes, meaning that completing some levels may prove more difficult for some characters. Although there appears to be four characters, info from Diablo suggest that there may be a hidden one lurking around somewhere.

As Sonic, the aim was simply to complete the first level of the game. Rushing through the level was quite exhilarating, as it all ran quite smoothly. Sonic looked better than ever, through a jungle type level, killing off mutant pink crabmeat. Ah, well, a first time for everything I s'pose. ^_^

One dissapointment I must comment on, and this is in no way said to degrade the already impressive graphics in the demo. But, you can't help feeling somewhat bemused about the SAGE screenshot, as the graphics in the SAGE shot looks a lot better than the actual playable demo. Whether it was a still screenshot that Diablo put together so that the finished game would look like the shot above or what still puzzles me, but the graphics are less detailed than in the SAGE screen, great as the visuals are already. Maybe I'm getting ahead of myself, the game's only about halfway done.

Other slight niggles are only cosmetic, they aren't really crucial to the game's success, because the playability is up there with the best of them, as is the action. You do feel slightly jaded once you see the SAGE shot, then discover the current rings are twice the size of your head though, plus while playing as Sonic, his running animation seemed... wooden. The rest of the animations were fine, it's just the running thing. But those things aren't important, it's just me trying to pick at holes. Of course, I try to pick holes in every upcoming game, I can't give a crap game 5/5 - but when it came to this game my little nit-picking only came down to small, tiny tiny graphic niggles, they aren't even problems.

Sonic S-V 2 looks great at the stage it is going right now, and more features that will appear come final test version should be; 24+ levels, a cast of 10 characters - 5 playable, each character having 6-8 rounds plus bosses, Boss rounds including the rather cool sounding Chrome Cannon, a miniture Tamagotchi style Chao garden, save passwords, and it looks as if there's room for two players too. Hopefully Dreadknux should get a full version to toy around with to give you a full hands-on review, when Diablo finishes his game and gives me the link to the final version. Magic.
