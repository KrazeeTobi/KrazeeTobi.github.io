1996
The year of cancellations!

<<1991 | 1992 | 1993 | 1994 | 1995 | 1996 | 1997 | 1998 | 1999 | 2000 | 2001>>

1996 was better than the last year for Sonic, many games were announced by Sega that just might save the life of the Saturn and give a great Goodbye to the Mega Drive. Some titles were not released, or cancelled due to one reason or another, but there was a surprise Sonic release that hit the nation...:

Sonic the Fighters - Arcade/Sega Saturn
June 1996

This appeared in Japanese Arcades and was quite successful, other than the Arcades it made an appearance on Sega TV, but was never released on the Saturn as Sega had hoped to. It was a great Arcade fighting game and involved Sonic, Amy, Knuckles, Metallix, Bean, Bark upon many others.

Sonic X-Treme - Never Released
Canned Game

This ever so good looking game was planned for a Saturn release, this was to be the Sonic Adventure, only on 32-Bit. The world was totally 3D, rotating cameras, free platforming, the lot. Features that we could have been expecting was the Spin Slash, demonstrated in the screenshot, throwing rings and much more. Due to development problems Sega canned this and made Sonic 3D Blast instead.

See more information on Sonic X-Treme - BETA Game Testing

Sonic 3D Blast - Mega Drive/Sega Saturn
July 1996

This was Sega's resort after they had terminal difficulties developing Sonic X-Treme. It wasn't in proper 3D - instead the gameplay was at an angle, but there were polygons involved. Although many people were disappointed about X-Treme, Sonic 3D was a great game in its own right, and Sega was able to release on both Saturn and Mega Drive.

See more information on Sonic 3D Blast - Mega Drive/Saturn

Sonic Blast - Master System/Game Gear
July 1996

A surprise release on the Master System, about three years after the last Sonic game, Sega send out Sonic Blast, which is a platformer starring Sonic, but you can also play as Knuckles. It really is quite amazing for any 8-Bit machine to do what Sega have done to Sonic Blast, its really worth checking out. The very last Sonic game to appear on the Master Syatem, and also the final outing for the blue blur on the Game Gear too.

See more information on Sonic Blast - MS/GG
