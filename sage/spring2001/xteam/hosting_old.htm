Rules & Regulations
Please read this page carefully to ensure that you understand these rules and regulations. If you do not agree to these, please delete your page immediatly.

1. Being on the team

When being a member of the SSRG staff team, you are expected to hold your best efforts at hand. We expect as little laziness as possible. If you're site isn't up to standards, we will notify you to ask that you please fix it and you will be given a certain amount of time to fix it. If you do not choose to keep up the work we expect from you otherwise, your account will be deleted. We try to treat all our members with the very best respect that we can offer and respectivley, we don't like to be unfair to anyone. So, we expect full respect from you as well. We used to have a requirement where updating the site once a week was at hand. However, while this rule would help draw visitors to the site on a timely manor, it became silly to watch an update that could normally occur in one day be spead out over several days. So, we don't ask that your page be updated every day, but we expect you to keep working on something related to website assignment. You should do this in a timely manor that is convenient to you. Keep in mind that we do go through the sites avalible on SSRG on a regular basis for quality control. If we are able to ask you "why this and that has not been updated for X amount of time", then you have a problem.

You must also be serious about the site or project you want to have hosted on SSRG. If your plan is to just have a site on SSRG so you can brag to your friends, don't bother e-mailing us. We have hosted far too many lazy people who claim that they will start a kick-butt site only to do little or nothing and then disappear a few weeks later. It would be another story if your project was truely done. People who do this waste our time from doing something more importand, like search for betas. =) SSRG is about hosting the best of the best Sonic the Hedgehog related projects and not every STH website on the net.

 

2. "Time Capsule" rule

If you feel that your project is done and there is nothing left to do and/or you want to move on to do other things, let us know. We will surely honor your request to leave and we will be more than willing to keep your site on the server permanently if you so choose.

 

3. Requirements and what we expect

There are no true requirements. In general, we will accept any project or website that relates to Sonic the Hedgehog. However, no fan stuff such as an MP3 archive, a fan fiction archive, etc. If you want to run a site like that, talk to the guys at Sonichq.org.

Which sites/projects do we accept and which ones do we reject is based apon the simple procedure of "if we like it, we will host it". If we don't really care for it, we won't host it. The key is originality. If your goal is to mirror a section already on SSRG but under a different name, don't bother e-mailing us. After all, does it make sense to have two sections that cover the same exact things?

However, if you feel that a section on SSRG is slacking and that you feel that you can be of major help to improve that section on SSRG, e-mail us. Perhaps the two of you could work together. I prefer to see people work TOGETHER instead of AGAINST each other. Collaboration helps speed research and certainly it yields better content.

 

4. What we need from you -- proof

You must provide us with either screen shots or the project itself to us to prove to us that you are indeed doing what you claim to be doing. As for the website, give us a URL to your site instead and we will review it. It is much help to give us some details on the stuff you send us as we talk about your project so that we are more aware of what it will be like.

 

5. Our responsibility and yours

We are not responsible for any files that have been deleted from your workspace, unless however you have broken a rule posted on this page. Do NOT give your username or password to ANYONE. What we ARE responsible for is keeping you at home and ensuring that you are happy. That is out ONLY responsibility. The rest is yours. Of course though, all of that is very basic common sense, we want to make sure you understand this anyhow.
