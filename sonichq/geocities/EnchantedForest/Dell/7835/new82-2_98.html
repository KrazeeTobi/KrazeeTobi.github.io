8-23-98 Return Of The Hedgehog Part 2.
 Now that we finally saw the actual Sonic Adventure, we can have a look at what this game will do. First off, this game is the best looking graphical game to date, NO other game can compare. Even games from the arcades have trouble. And from the looks of things, the Sonic Team did a great job in keeping Sonic's super speed. They solved the exploring part by letting other slower characters do it. This game might just be revolutionary. The characters will be able to interact and make enemies and friends with other minor characters in the game called Choas. Then at times, your new *friends* will help you out. Now, think about this, this is only a FIRST generation game for the Dreamcast, and it's already running at 60fps and 640*480 resolution with about 1.5+million polygons per second. Think of the Sonic sequels behind it and a few years down the line! After Sonic Team make NiGHTS, they might just turn back and make a Sonic Adventure 2. If they don't do it, it's a sure bet that Traveler's Tale will make a Sonic game (Sonic R 2?). Since I AM a Sega fan, this is my point of view: with Sonic Adventure's showing Sega IS back. Sonic should be the BIG game of the next year, beating out other games like Zelda and Sega's own powerful line ups. With the announcement of Sonic Adventure, everyone's favorite hedgehog is officially back on the game scene! Long live the hedgehog!

8-22-98 Breaking News: Sonic Adventure - How Long?!
 Now that we have seen the game and learned the game play, another question comes to mind: "How long's the game?!" Right now some are saying 7-10 levels, others are saying 36 acts in 18 zones. 8-22-98 Breaking News: More On Sonic Adventure's Levels!
 Here's more info about some of them that were shown at the show. But this is just a small part of the whole game. Source: Game-Online.

Ice Cap Zone - A good old snow level set on mountains with avalanches and Sonic doing snowboarding. The snow and ice in the background are very detailed.

Red Mountain - A desert stage also involving a mine section. Perfect for Knuckles. A lot of sharp spikes in this level, and if you're a person who's afraid of heights, this level's not for you. Now unlike the previous games, if you fall off a bridge of the edge, you'll fall a long way...

Lost World - A mysterious jungle, set below towering mountain. A chance for the Dreamcast to show off its amazing vistas. And by the look of it, this is probably the most detailed zone.

Inca - The level inspired by Sonic Team's expedition to South America. An architectural marvel, with huge trap-littered temples to explore.

Speed Highway - Classic Sonic territory - an incredible neon city for Sonic to rip through, defying gravity, running down the sides of skyscrapers dodging civic scenery - brilliant fountains - traffic. The buildings in this level look just incredible! I've never seen such details in any other game!

Flying Section - This is not the official title, but describes one of the key sub-levels, where Sonic and Tails return to their bi-plane from Sonic 2 and engage in some Panzer Dragoon-style shoot 'em up action - facing 'Chaos' (that water dragon) in the air.

That's all for now, we'll bring you more when more information becomes available.

8-22-98 Breaking News: Sonic Adventure's Worlds Are HUGE!
 Here's What Yuji Naka said about it:

I’ve put huge efforts into creating a big map. Of course, I have to focus on the details of the 3D world, but I have to create a vast huge scale map as well. This is the most difficult aspect of Sonic Adventure. If it were Mario or a slower character, I wouldn’t have to create maps quite as large as this. I created the huge world first for Sonic, but then thought, why not utilise these maps for other characters? Maybe I could add some variety to the game - not just running quickly through this huge world. That’s one of the key concepts in the game.

And he also said that this is the biggest and fastest action game ever made! Must be, because last I heard, Sonic Team trippled in size from 20 to 60 members!

8-22-98 Breaking News: Sonic Adventure Gameplay!

You might have seen an Abyss-style lizard/water hybrid creature in some of the leaked Sonic Adventure materials. That’s Chaos. Robotnik has been feeding him with crystals to boost his power, and now it’s time for Sonic to step into the fray to defeat Chaos, his minions and of course, Dr Robotnik himself.
