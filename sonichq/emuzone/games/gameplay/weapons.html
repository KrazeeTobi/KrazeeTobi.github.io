Ring Items




Power Rings
There are 5 natural elements on Mobius - Earth, Water, Wind, Fire - and the Power Ring! Sonic doesn't have an energy meter, and since Power Rings are found in many places on Planet Mobius, its these rings that Sonic catches to keep himself full of energy. Hit a monitor box with a ring in it and you are rewarded with 10 rings! Collecting 100 rings rewards Sonic with an extra life, and 50 rings plus 7 Chaos Emeralds will get Sonic access to his "Super" form. HOWEVER there is one catch - Whenever Sonic gets hit by a badnik or lands on spikes he loses his rings, and if you are hit with no rings in your possesion then you lose 1 life...

Dimensional Rings
First introduced in Sonic 1, Dimensional Rings are used as an alternate means of transportation to the Special Zone or other strange places!

Dimensional Rings are MUCH LARGER than regular Power Rings.

The Dimension Rings were first introduced in Sonic 1. In this game, they were always found at the end of an act, and only appeared when you had finished the act with at least 50 rings. This type of Dimensional Ring was also used to access the special stages in Sonic CD and Knuckles' Chaotix.

In Sonic 3 & Knuckles, another type of Dimensional Ring was introduced. Every act up to the Hidden Palace Zone had at least 2 Dimensional Rings hidden within. In Knuckles' Chaotix, hidden Dimensional Rings, which only appearred if you had at least 20 rings, took you to the Bonus Zone instead of the Special Zone.

A third type of dimension ring appeared in Sonic 3D Blast - these Dimensional Rings can be used to safely transport Flicky birds to their home dimension.

Blue Rings (Knuckles Chaotix Only)
Don't you just HATE IT when you lose all of your rings after getting hit by a badnik? First introduced in Knuckles Chaotix, the Blue Rings can automatically solve that problem. The Blue Rings bond all of your regular rings into a single large ring. These work like "backup shields," so it's best to try to have both a shield and a blue ring whenever possible.

With the Blue Power Ring, antime you get hit by a badnik and don't have a shield, you lose your blue ring and the bonding goes in to effect. Instead of losing ALL YOUR RINGS, you only lose 1 Large Ring - thus making it easier to collect! The large ring bounces slowly for about 5 seconds before splitting apart into many rings that bounce away as they usually do. Collect the large ring in time and you will have the complete total of rings, just like you did before!

Chaos Rings (Knuckles Chaotix Only)
First introduced in Knuckles Chaotix, Chaos Rings are part of what fuels The Carnival Island and can be collected in the 3D Special Stages of the game!

The Chaos Rings, in sequence, are gold, light blue, green, magenta, purple, and dark blue. Robotnik already has the huge "Master Ring" in his possession. At the end of the game, he uses its energy to turn Metal Sonic into Hyper Metal Sonic - a giant red version that is so large that its head barely fits on the screen!

Ring Tether (Knuckles Chaotix Only)
First introduced in Knuckles Chaotix, Ring Tethers allow 2 characters to become connected kind of like a rubber band that snaps together! This allows them to perform special moves and even throw another character at a badnik to destroy them!

There are 5 common moves that you can do with the Tether Rings:

1. The basic use of the Ring Tether is to have one character run in a direction while the other digs in with his shoes and stays in one place (a "hold"). Eventually, the energy between the rings will glow red and the 2 characters will be unable to move further apart. At this point, the holding character can stop holding, and both will run forward at great speed.
2. Alternatively, the running character can snap back towards the holding one, retaining all of his speed but moving in the opposite direction. You can release the hold button and both characters will run in the direction that the running character is moving in.
3. Toss your partner onto a high ledge or upper floor of the level. When he lands, have him hold there, and he'll pull you up if you stretch the rings apart and release.
4. Pick up your partner and you will be able to run without the bungee effect, sometimes making control a little easier. Your partner will spin, so most badniks will be destroyed if you run into them. The disadvantage is that you can't use your special moves while carrying your partner.
5. The most difficult move is "flying." Toss the second character into the air, and have him hold as he reaches the peak of his flight. You'll start to snap up, and you should press up while this happens. Your partner will snap up above you. Press hold again. Keep repeating this and you can theoretically fly as high as you want, but it's difficult to maintain. Practice this move in the Isolated Island Zone. It's not necessary for completing the game, but it will help you to find some hidden areas.

Chaos Emeralds




The Chaos Emeralds
Chaos Emeralds are possibly the most POWERFUL source of raw abundant energy on the planet - and unfortunately Doctor Robotnik is ALWAYS after them. Chaos Emeralds MORE THEN LIKELY are found in the Special Zone, but rarer ones have been known to exist on the surface of Mobius. 7 Chaos Emeralds plus 50 power rings will grant thier owners access to the "Super Mode". These baubles of infinite energies possess mysterious powers and its with these that Robotnik wishes to power his most vicious creations! Chaos Emeralds also can exist in colors OTHER than green as well, though Green Emeralds are well known to represent a "universal color". In the games, the Emeralds have had many varying colors. As of recent (Sonic Adventure) these have been white, red, yellow, purple, aqua, green, and blue. Other known colors have been pink as well as the Gray Emerald - which was said to have some link in the creation of Doctor Robotnik...

The Super Emeralds
So far - only 7 Super Emeralds have been known to exist and only in 1 place: The Floating Island. These 7 "super" emeralds are larger versions of the regular Emeralds and surround the Master Emerald in The Chaos Chamber of The Floating Islands - Hidden Palace Zone...These emeralds can be used to access the Special Zone if you stand on top of them for a period of time...7 Chaos Emeralds plus 7 Super Emeralds and 50 rings will grant you access to "Hyper Mode", where you characters are now twice as powerful as they were before...

The Master Emerald
Most likely The Master Emerald is the LARGEST Chaos Emerald in existance and is solely responsible for keeping the Floating Island afloat. When Doc Botniks Death Egg crashed into the island, it was this that The Fat Guy wished to use as fuel for his ship! This is also the item that Knuckles is sworn to guard as he does the entire island itself - for if the Master Emerald is ever removed from its pedestal, it would only be a matter of time before the Floating Island crashed into the sea...

The Time Stones
These odd crystals reside on the Little Planet and seem to be the key to utilizing its unique temporal properties.

Items And Obstacles




Spin Gates And Prison Eggs
Spingates and Prison Eggs are always located at the end of a zone just before you proceed to the next one. Located in practically every Sonic game - run through the spingate to signify that you've completed the level! If you find a falling one, sometimes you can get it to land in a certain area to recieve a powerup! Usually located in the final act for a zone, you'll find a Prison Egg. Hit the plunger at the top of it to free the captive animals!

Air Bubbles
First introduced in Sonic 1, Air bubbles can be used in 1 of 2 ways! In the regular Sonic games you MUST have these to stay alive! Watch for air fissures in the ground and catch one quickly before the countdown ends and you drown! In Game Gear games use these to get out of deep places in the water by floating upwards out of deep underwater subterranean places!

Buttons
Located throught a board, look for buttons that are scattered along a level to open up closed passageways or clear an area for you !




The Tornado
First introduced in Sonic 2, the Tornado is probably the most used vehicle in Sonic's arsenal! With Tails at the wheel and Sonic riding atop its wing, the two use the plane to travel to faraway locations that can normally not be reached on foot. The little bi-plane also comes equipped with a lot of options such as its ability to morph (Sonic Adventure) and the Rocket Boosters that come out of its side slots !

The Sea Fox (Tails Only)
First introduced in Sonic Triple Trouble, the Sea Fox is possibly one of the BEST Sonic devices yet...A full submarine complete with its own oxygen tank and gun turrets! The Sea Fox is a MUST HAVE when using Tails! Just make sure it doesn't get hit too much...






Regular And Portable Springboard
There are 2 types of Springboards - regular springboards and portable springboards (which can be used as weapons See below), each Springboard basically serves only one purpose - which is to increase and highten jumping ability. First introduced in Sonic Chaos, these are the second types of Springboards which release themselves from the ground instead of staying stuck in the ground like other items. Instead of Sonic or whomever bouncing from them into the sky, the springboard will bounce along with the player, and also kill a badnik or two along the way!

Lampposts And Star Posts
First introduced in Sonic 1, Star Posts serve as halfway points to the Sonic world. Whenever you lose a man, you will instantly start at the last Star Post you crossed as present time and score are saved at these areas - unfortunately, you start at Star Posts with 0 rings. Star Posts are sometimes places in strategic points on the levels - halfway points and near bosses. Also - Just like Dimensional Rings - Star Posts also help Sonic and company gain access to the Special Zone and The Bonus Stages...

Bumper Blocks
First introduced in Sonic 1, Bumpers come in many shapes and sizes, though the most common is a circle or sphere with a star on it. Some alternate versions are the many polygon-shaped bumpers in Casino Night and balloons in Speed Slider. Bumpers are plentiful in Spring Yard, Casino Night, Collision Chaos, Carnival Night, Speed Slider, Spring Stadium, and the Sonic 3 Special Zone.

Reverse And Goal Blocks
Located in the Sonic 1 Special Stages and the Sonic3 bonus areas, Reverse Blocks shift the room so that instead of tilting one way, it tilts another, and if a Goal Block is touched you will istantly leave the level whether you have the Chaos Emerald or not. Avoid these two blocks in the Special Stages...

Monitor Boxes




The Monitor Boxes !!!
Located across various zones are the monitor boxes - filled with the various power ups Sonic, Knuckles, and Tails will need to give them that edge across the badniks! From the many different versions of the Power Shields to the Power Sneakers and other items, monitor boxes are the quick and easy way to get the upper hand! Listed in order from left to right on the picture above you will find out what each box icon stands for. Those that are not shown above are shown below including a picture unless otherwise stated...

  • Super Ring -- Pop this box to recieve 10 Power Rings in your inventory.
  • Blue Ring -- Adds the Blue Ring to your inventory.


  • Power Shield -- Pop this box to recieve the regular shield! With this you are free against 1 attack without losing your rings!
  • Flame Shield -- Pop this box to recieve a flame shield and access to the Flame Dash - one of the Special moves! With this shield you can be incincible to all fire attacks! The shield will dissipate if you hit water or electricity.
  • Electric Shield -- Pop this box to recieve a electric shield and access to the Double Jump - one of the Special moves! With this shield you can be incincible to all electricity attacks! The shield will dissipate if you hit water or fire.
  • Water Shield -- Pop this box to recieve a water shield and access to the Bounce Attack - one of the Special moves! With this shield you can have permanent air underwater and reflect most bullets by underwater enemies! The shield will dissipate if you hit electricity or fire.
  • Invincibility -- Pop this box to recieve temporary invincibilty against all attacks - except for getting crushed or drowning.
  • Power Sneakers -- After popping a monitor box with this icon, Sonic can grab the Power Sneakers! Grabbing the Power Sneakers enhances Sonic's running ability as now he becomes TWICE as fast as his normal speed


  • Rocket Sneakers -- First introduced in Sonic Chaos, these are the second types of Power Sneakers, in which Sonic will gain rocket boosters on his sneaks and fly in a straight line varied path for a limited time. Perfect for overcoming large pits and spike filled areas , and getting out of troublesome spots


  • Extra Life -- Pop this box to recieve an extra life! Life can go up to from 9 to 99 men maximum.
  • Rotate Characters -- In Knuckles' Chaotix, this causes your partner to become the player character, while the player character becomes computer-controlled (or controlled by the second player, if you are playing a 2-player game). The effect is temporary, but allows you to use different specialty moves for a while.
  • Change Character -- These monitors show each of the faces of the 5 characters that you are not controlling. For example, if you play as Vector and Espio, it shows Knux, Mighty, Charmy, Heavy, and Bomb. The picture on the monitor constantly rotates from one character to the next. Hitting the monitor will temporarily make the pictured character your partner.
  • Giant -- A monitor with a large blue arrow. Popping it will scale your character to an enormous height, or cancel the effects of a "Tiny" monitor. The extra jumping height can be handy, but it's much harder to avoid badniks. More of a novelty than anything useful. You should be cautious until it wears off or you can find a "Tiny" monitor.
  • Tiny -- A monitor with a small yellow arrow. Popping it will scale your character down to a diminutive height, or cancel the effects of a "Giant" monitor. You can't really do much until it wears off, because your jump height and speed will be tiny as well. Mostly a novelty, it's only useful purpose is to cancel the "Giant" monitor's effects. You should be cautious until it wears off or you can find a "Giant" monitor.
  • Zone Select -- It's not really found in a monitor, but it is listed in your inventory with items that you got from monitors. This very useful item is found in the Bonus Zone and gives you total control over which zone you go to next from the World Entrance Zone Select in Knuckles' Chaotix.
  • Character Select -- Similar to the Zone Select item, but it gives you total control in choosing the character who will be the computer-controlled player in the next act. It's not as useful as the Zone Select, since choosing the character you want is pretty easy.


  • Blast Shield -- Pop this box to recieve the Blast Shield and the Blast Attack! The Blast Attack allows Sonic to literally home in on the enemy! Just jump in the air and press A!
  • Robotnik Monitor -- Popping one of these boxes will have the same effect as a hit from a badnik. Try to avoid them completely.
  • Teleport -- Found in Sonic 2 in the 2-player Competition or as an available item in Debug Mode, this item switches the positions of Sonic and Tails.
  • The Other Blue Ring -- Found only as an item in Sonic CD's Debug Mode, it is just like a Super Ring Monitor, but it gives you 50 rings instead.
  • Super Mode -- Found only as an available item in the Debug Modes of Sonic 3 & Knuckles, Sonic 3, and Sonic & Knuckles, this monitor has a glowing "S" on it. It gives you 50 rings and causes you to become Super.

Tails' Adventure




Tails' Items !!!
Being a major switch from traditional Sonic games, Tails Adventure chooses to go the route of the puzzler over action. In fact, it might even be considered an action-RPG in 2D platformer style. Starting the game off, Tails has nothing more than Regular Bombs, a 10-hit power bar (hits being measured in Rings, of course), and about two seconds on his Flight Guage. As the player travels through the game, he obtains a total of 26 weapons, about twelve seconds of flight time, and a 99-Ring power bar. Weapons are discovered hidden around levels, while power bar and Flight Guage are extended by discovering Chaos Emeralds (there are six in total), also hidden around the levels. As Tails' inventory increases, only four weapons can be brought into any level at a time. Often this presents a trial and error setup, requiring several trips into any one level to determine which four weapons need to be brought along for the trip. (The most useful items tend to be the Triple Bomb, Remote Bomb, Remote Robot, and Teleport Device.) And now here's a list of the game's 26 items and their individual effects:

  • Regular Bomb -- You start the game with these babies, and they're presumably Tails' key form of offense. Blows up all enemies and your basic stone barriers.
  • Large Bomb -- Destroys all on-screen enemies, but takes several seconds to detonate after placing. Also useful for those stubborn, thick stone barriers.
  • Remote Bomb -- This handy little sucker will only detonate when you trigger it. Not very effective against enemies, but a necessity on many puzzles. Carry this item with you into nearly every level, as it's almost always needed...
  • Napalm Bomb -- Throw this puppy on the ground to create a wave of flame that destroys everything in its path, including objects above. It's the only way to clear through tall grass.
  • Triple Bomb -- An enhanced version of the Regular Bomb. Once you obtain this item, you'll never need the Regular Bombs again. Naturally, being Tails' key form of offense, you'll need these in every level.
  • Wrench -- The final obtainable item in the game, the Wrench's only use comes in the final level, where you'll be using it to work machines and reverse the direction of conveyor belts.
  • Helmet -- By ducking under this giant bowl, all enemy fire will be reflected, and Tails will be safe from any damage. Of course, you can't move at all while using it...
  • Remote Robot -- The most important item in the game, barring only the Regular and Triple Bombs. Once you snag this mechanical pup, pack him along to every level. 90% of the game's puzzles revolve around maneuvering the robot to open the path for Tails himself, so learn this canine's tricks early.
  • Hammer -- Take out your frustrations on enemies and certain stone barriers with this fine mallet. (Bean's bombs, Amy's mallet...what's next, Sonic's spin?)
  • Teleport Device -- This is a handy little item that should prove extremely useful while exploring new levels to determine what items you'll need to pack along, as well as when returning to old levels to pick up items you couldn't reach the first time. Warps you back to Tails' House anytime.
  • Night Vision -- You'll need these infra-red goggles to light up dark levels. I mean, unless you think you're good enough to play them in pitch black...
  • Speed Boots -- Anyone who's ever played a Sonic game in his life (and I don't think you'd be here if you haven't) should be familiar with this item. Gives Tails the ability to run, and fly at a faster speed.
  • Super Glove -- You can't have a self-respecting action-RPG without a Power Glove item, which naturally allows you to lift obscenely heavy objects. It's yet to be explained exactly how wearing a glove raises a character's strength, but there are some questions better left unasked...
  • Fang Doll -- Better known to US audiences as "Nack". Everyone's favorite weasel gives Tails a lesson in Ring-pinching. Carry this item along with you into any level to increase the chances that a destroyed enemy will drop a Ring.
  • Knuckles Doll -- Bombs are OK, but sometime's it's just more satisfying to belt those bent birds' beaks in (there's a new tongue-twister for ya). So make like everyone's favorite echidna and implant your fist into some face.
  • Sonic Doll -- ...I had to say something. Tails borrows Sonic's spindash with this item, although the control is more like that of a slingshot. Like Knuckles' punch, it's more of a cute series tie-in than a useful item.
  • Item Radar -- This time-saving piece of equipment will let you know if there's an item you missed still in the level. If you don't get a read on the radar, don't waste your time.
  • Radio -- While completely useless to gameplay, this beat-buster is the game's only trace of a sound test. It just so happens that you can continue the level while jamming to your choice of tune...
  • Proton Torpedo -- The Proton Torpedo is to the Sea Fox what the Regular Bombs are to Tails. Your standard underwater weapon, they fire straight ahead.
  • Vulcan Gun -- A smaller, rapid-fire version of the Proton Torpedo. This is particularly handy in the Battle Fortress dogfight...
  • Extra Speed -- Created solely for Lake Crystal, this boost-builder is the only way to get up that level's waterfalls.
  • Extra Armor -- A starry forcefield protects the Sea Fox, making it invincible as long as this item is selected.
  • Anti-air Missile -- This projectile shoots straight upward...handy for surprising enemies from below, and busting open barriers above.
  • Spark -- Creates a strong electrical field around the Sea Fox, clearing the screen of all enemies and mines.
  • Mine -- Like the Anti-air Missile, except you drop 'em below rather than shoot 'em upward. Catch enemies from above, or destroy barriers directly below.
  • Rocket Booster -- This attachment allows the Sea Fox to fly...the only way to play Battle Fortress 1.
