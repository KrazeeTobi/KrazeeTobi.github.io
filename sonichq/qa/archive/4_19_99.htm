More Questions

Dear Sonic HQ,
  Well, it's been a while since I've e-mailed you guys. Since I've been getting more of Sonic & Knuckles, a lot of questions I would ask you guys have been answered. Now, I'll take it upon myself to ask some interesting questions for this day and age (I sound like a geeky prophet).

1.) Ok, so far Sega won't allow Sonic and Sally to get married or date. If we will never see them do that stuff, who would you think they would let Sonic marry or date?
2.) Like question # 1, who will Knuckles marry or date in the end? Julie-su is nice, but I thought she was created be Archie Comics.
3.) When would we likely see Big the Cat, E-102, and Perfect Chaos in Archie's Sonic comics?
4.) When will Amy Rose develop her Sonic Adventure look for Archie's Sonic comics?
5.) This question has been bugging me for a while, but who created Scratch: Sega or DiC Entertainment?
6.) Ok, we all know the third season of Sonic the Hedgehog won't happen. My question is how come Sega didn't blow the whistle on Sonic Underground yet? I thought DiC France made up Manic, Sonia, Sleet, and Dingo?
7.) How's the Fleetway Sonic comics going along overseas? I hear that its been going downhill.

Well, if I have any questions, I know where to look. Also, sorry about the controversy about Tails' age that I started. I regret starting that up. I just hope it's finished.

Christopher

P.S. If you guys, though you don't have a reason to, have any artwork you want to submit for my site, e-mail me at ahe@webzone.net.

1. Only if he gets married or starts dating in the games first. It would be foolish for them to let Archie write in a marriage without getting approval from SoJ first.
2. Sega allows more freedom with Knuckles because he isn't the company mascot. The "First Date" comics storyline starts with Knux #26. However, for marriage, Knux would probably be subject to the same rules as Sonic.
3. The SA adaptation will be somewhere around Sonic #78-82. SSS #12 and Knux #34-37 will also cover SA.
4. That hasn't been revealed yet.
5. Probably DiC.
6. It seems strange, after all we've heard about SEGA vs. Archie, but SEGA has said that they're satisfied with SU. I don't think that SEGA's problems with Archie really had anything to do with the fact that the comic has a lot of non-SEGA characters. I think they were more worried about Sonic and a non-SEGA character in love, and about the fact that the SatAM storyline had a very different atmosphere than the games. The first issue obviously won't exist with SU, and perhaps they feel that SU's atmosphere is close enough.
7. Yeah, it has been going downhill. There's a special report about Fleetway management on the News Interviews page.

Diamond Dust Zone

In Diamond Dust Zone, Act 1, there is a part where you get the first five Flickies, how do you get the rest? (I suppose it may be a breakable wall). Thanks.

Kyle Wightman

Sorry, I'm kinda confused by your question. After you get 5 Flickies, just find a Giant Ring like you did in the previous zones. You'll go to another area with 5 more Flickies. If you have trouble finding all of them, it's probably because some of them are in Snowman badniks. Find a Snowman in a green hat that slides around and shoots at you. Spin Attack it, and a Flicky in an ice cube will remain. Attack the ice cube and the Flicky will be freed.

Secret Levels

could you tell me how to access the secret levels on sonic 1,2+3 if there are any
bye matty

There aren't any secret levels in Sonic 1.
The beta version of Sonic 2 had the Hidden Palace Zone, Wood Zone, and Genocide City Zone, but all were removed in the final version. The level data for Hidden Palace actually still exists, and you can play the level with a Pro Action Replay. Unfortunately, you can't finish it without Debug mode. You can find out more in the FAQ.
Sonic 3's secret levels are the ones that are added to the game when you lock-on with Sonic & Knuckles.
