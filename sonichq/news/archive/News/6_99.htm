SSS#11. - Zifei 2:43pm CST, 6-25-99
Recently, Ken Penders told the comic new media about Super Sonic Special #11:

Sonic Super Special #11 ships on Oct. 1 from Archie Comics. The "all-female" issue is written by Ken Penders.

"After hearing way too many people say Sonic and Knuckles comics are aimed strictly at boys, it occurred to me that here was a chance to acknowledge our female readership, and so I concocted a theme issue aimed squarely at placing the female cast members front and center," Penders told The Continuum.

"I picked five of the readers' favorite characters for the spotlight, and to make the Special really special, so to speak, I'd feature all five in solo stories where something pivotal happens to each of the main characters. Every story has an effect, a direct consequence, upon the main continuity in each series that simply can't be ignored, and will be expanded upon in future issues.

"Having written all five stories, it also occurred to me that it might be a kick having each story pencilled by someone different, while I had the fun of inking every one, so I rounded up a list of suspects, which included Jim Valentino, James Fry, Colleen Doran and Sonic cover artist Pat Spaziante. I saved the fifth story for myself, as that featured a character I created."

Bi-monthly Knuckles... - Zifei 12:43pm CST, 6-25-99
Knuckles will go bi-monthly starting around K#35 due to poor sales. Here's what Ken has to say regarding this matter:

After KNUCKLES #34, Knuckles will become a bi-monthly series (more than likely) as opposed to even 9 times a year (still under consideration). What would have been KNUCKLES #35 will now be SONIC #82, just so readers won't have to wait an extra month for the conclusion to the SONIC ADVENTURE game adaptation.

Before anyone makes a claim if we had done this or that, things would be different, let me relate some FACTS, as these are indesputable:

FACT ONE: SONIC continues to outsell KNUCKLES, by a more than comfortable margin. KNUCKLES is clearly on the bubbble. However, the latest sales results are from issue #22, so any efforts fandom is doing to save this book as far as sales go won't be know for at least another four or five months at least.

FACT TWO: Changing artists had no effect whatsoever in either the decision making process or on sales. KNUCKLES sales begin declining during a popular storyline while Manny was still regular artist. (The reasons for declining sales can probably -- and more like are -- due to poor distribution, but a poor name recognition in the marketplace is also being attributed. Sonic fandom very well knows who Knuckles is, but the general audience does not.)

FACT THREE: Despite all the complaints over how lousy things supposedly are with the SONIC series and all the accolades over how well-done (for the most part) KNUCKLES is, sales do not support the contention that fans are unahppy with SONIC and happy with KNUCKLES. Again, one can argue quality until one is blue in the face, but the fact is a book sells for reasons beyond whether it's good or not. For example, there are several years worth of various Marvel and DC titles of some of the most popular characters -- Batman, Superman, Spider-Man, Fantastic Four, X-Men to name but a few - where the content has been totally substandard, especially in comparison to the good stuff, yet people still kept buying them. Why? Brand name has something to do with it. Sonic is a recognizable brand name. Knuckles, in all honesty, is not.

So what does this mean?

It means that unless more people support this book, it's gonna go bye-bye.

What can you do?

What I've recommended before, tell friends, and even write the publisher Michael Silberkleit and tell him how much you enjoy reading the series.

It's now or never, and only you guys can do it. Please spread the word.

Well, we can only hope that in a few month, sale figures will pick up.

    Ken On Robotnik Again... - Zifei 12:38am CST, 6-5-99
    Ken Penders again confirmed that the Robotnik we used to know is dead. Here's what he said:

    Sure thing. Robotnik IS dead. Yet we're promoting the return of Robotnik. How can this be? Without giving away any surprises, we had this planned for quite some time. Sega gave us permission to kill off Robotnik as long as we brought him back some time later. They were figuring on six months later. We were already figuring out how to fight a guerilla war to keep him out of action longer than that. The major reason we did so is that we never felt that particular version was believable as Sonic's ultimate villain. We wanted something closer in concept to the games and the anime, something that would really make the Sonic fans go wild. Personally, I like what Karl has done, but the ultimate jury has yet to be heard from.

    Gee...I wonder what that meant...
