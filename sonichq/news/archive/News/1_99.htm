Sonic And Garfield Pack! - Zifei 3:16pm CST, 1-26-99
We first reported some times ago, but Sega PC just released the Sonic And Garfield Pack for Windows 95/98. This game CD will include Sonic and Knuckles Collection, Garfield - Caught in the Act, and Baku Baku - a puzzle game. The CD retails for only $29.99, that's about the same price as Sonic 3D Blast PC. Below is the official press release:

SAN FRANCISCO--Jan. 26, 1999--Sega(R) PC today released "Sonic and Garfield Pack(tm)," which brings speedy Sonic the Hedgehog(tm) and GARFIELD, the world's laziest cat, together to strut their stuff on one CD.

"Sonic and Garfield Pack" includes hit games from "Sonic and Knuckles Collection(tm)," "Garfield -- Caught in the Act(tm)" and a special bonus game -- "Baku Baku(tm)" -- which was named Computer Gaming World Magazine's 1997 "Classic/Puzzle Game of the Year."

"Sonic and Garfield Pack" gives gamers a total of more than 50 levels of exciting gameplay with well-known characters they love for the low price of $29.99.

More than half of the top selling software products last year were based on well-known characters and properties, according to PC Data. "Sonic and Garfield Pack" is another example of Sega PC's dedication to bringing fun gameplay and beloved character content to the mass market at a competitive price.

"Sonic and Garfield Pack" us comprised of four great games:

-- "Sonic the Hedgehog 3(tm)" and "Sonic and Knuckles(tm)" feature all the spin-dashing moves, catchy soundtracks and fast action that made them favorites among gamers of all skill levels.

-- "Garfield -- Caught in the Act," shows off GARFIELD's acting abilities in more than 20 zany movie sets. Gamers must help GARFIELD escape from his late-night television nightmare by defeating the evil Glitch, whom GARFIELD himself created when trying to fix his broken TV set.

-- "Baku Baku" is an addictive and award-winning column puzzle sensation that pits gamers against the computer or each other to stack up snacks as they fall so all the critters have access to their favorite munchies -- dogs won't chew through bananas to get to bones and rabbits won't gnaw through bones to get to carrots.

"Sonic and Garfield Pack" is available in stores now and operates on Windows 95(R) and 98(R) computers with minimum specifications of a 60 Mhz Intel Pentium(R), AMD K5(R) or Cyrix 6X86(R) processor, 16 MB of memory, SVGA with 256 colors and a Sound Blaster(R) compatible soundcard. "Sonic and Garfield Pack" will support 16-bit color and is compatible with a keyboard or any Windows 95 and 98 compatible game controller.

So far I've found that the following Sonic Simplenet sites, who had Sonic music on them, are missing:

Sonic Adventure

Sonic R 2
This game is unconfirmed but rumored a long time ago. It is very likely, for as of now, Traveler's Tales (the team that made 3D Blast and Sonic R) is very quiet about the games they're making for the Dreamcast.

Sonic/Knuckles RPG

Sonic Underground
Gamer's Republic magazine has said that SoA is planning to make a game based on the cartoon of Sonic Underground coming up this April. I really can't say anything about this because there is nothing to say except that...they're crazy!

Sonic the Fighters
Another rumored game. Though this is very unlikely, as StF was made for the Titan STV arcade board which was the counterpart for the Sega Saturn. So the game would look really bad graphically on the Dreamcast...

That's all the Sonic games (rumored/confirmed) that I have heard about. Any questions? Just email me from the email above.

Sega Is In Control! - WB 6:53pm CST, 1-9-99
Our WB asked Ken Penders, the writer for Knuckles, how far Sega of America extends its control over the contents in the comics published by Archie. Here's Ken's reply:

When I first started working on SONIC, my partner Mike and I would have to write a detailed outline for Sega to review before we went ahead and wrote a complete script on every story we submitted. While Karl and I still submit an outline for each issue these days, more often than not it's after the fact and for Sega's benefit so they have the reader's digest version allowing them to follow along. With some exceptions, there's little editorial interference, and certainly nothing like the old days. On KNUCKLES, I have virtually free rein. The last major change I can recall Sega requesting occured during the Lost Paradise storyline, when they wanted me to eliminate any references to Nazi Germany, no matter how allegorical they were presented. Fast forward to the current Dark Alliance storyline, chock full of Nazi references and symbolism.

As for SONIC, because he is the company mascot, that's what Sega pays more attention to, and is more quickly to pounce on Justin for if they're unhappy with something.

And Ken said "no" when asked "has the Archie crew ever met Bernie Stolar: the head of Sega Of America?"
