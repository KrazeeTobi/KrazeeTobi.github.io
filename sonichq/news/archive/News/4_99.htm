CSN Sonic Comic Gone... - Zifei 6:54pm CST, 4-28-99
Ken just told us to not to put up the Comic Shop News Sonic comic because:

I hate to say it, but it's NOT okay to run the CSN Sonic story for several reasons. One: you're infringing on the CSN's efforts to generate sales to retailers. While you the customer may get your copy for free, the retailer has to pay for his, and as a result, a lot of retailers choose not to carry it as they consider it an added expense with little return. By running the Sonic serial, as well as other series, the CSN hopes that reader demand will encourage more retailers to carry their periodical. Two: Archie intends to reprint this story somewhere down the road. By making the story available to a wide audience online risks cutting into those sales, something neither Archie or Sega wants to see.

Subsequently, anyone caught displaying this on their website risks serious legal action from the parties involved, as they would be required to do so in order to protect their overall interests.

When it comes to something like this, if you're asking for my permission, I can't give it, and may have to report it if asked. This may not be what you want to hear, but I don't want to see anyone get burned playing with fire, which this certainly is.

So we took the scans down. Don't want any legal trouble. Personally, I don't see how putting them up will destroy sales...anyway, I also think it is illegal for me to tell you that the picture can be viewed through a post on the Mobius Forum...

    Knuckles Comic Endangered! - Zifei 4:35pm CST, 4-14-99
    This will probably upset a lot of you, but, here's what Ken Penders said earlier today:

    I'm not one to normally do something like this, as I recognize immediately that some people will claim my motivation is strictly self-interest. To be honest, self-interest does play a part, but only to a certain extent. However, when the deck keeps geeting stacked against you, you tend to resort to everything and anything in the hope that something will work.

    The problem is this...because various suits have affected the way KNUCKLES is distributed, KNUCKLES is now on the endangered list, with the very real possibility of being published only nine times a year, maybe six, or even being outright cancelled.

    How can this be so? you ask. Let me explain.

    Months ago, some woman complained to a manager at a WalMart that her child bought a comic book with offensive material at his store. Rather than simply not carry the offending title, WalMart made the corporate-wide decision to not carry ANY comics altogether. This resulted in 10,000 less copies of KNUCKLES alone being sold.

    While some of those readers probably bought subscriptions, the majority went looking for other sources. Unfortunately, despite the overwhelming evidence that SONIC and KNUCKLES would only bring in more customers, many comics retailers still refused to increase their orders. As for other outlets, they've proven to be hit and miss due to various factors with newstand distribution.

    Which brings us to the second shoe being dropped: how the current system of newstand distribution could spell disaster for KNUCKLES fans.

    In case anyone hasn't noticed lately, there are more magazines being published than there is room in stores to display them for sale. That means if you wish to get your product to market, you have to jump through the hoops set up by newstand distributors. Recently, the distributors have decided to charge more money for less space. That means a publisher like Archie has to cough up more money to maintain every title on the newstand they currently publish, or...

    ...they could pay the same amount and get less shelf space, resulting in some books getting cancelled, and others scaled back in their frequency of publication.

    While SONIC has been a solid performer with name-brand recognition, for some reason, both KNUCKLES and SONIC SUPER SPECIAL have a harder time getting through the food chain and into the readers hands.

    Be that as it may, the fact is, the publishers don't care about the whys and wherefores. They're not looking for explanations. Either a book sells or it doesn't. While that may not be fair from a fan's point of view, this is how business is currently conducted in America today.

    Now that we've defined the problem, that various factors are making it more difficult than ever for KNUCKLES' fans to follow their hero, we obviously need a solution, and in this case, there's only one. The only one the publishers will listen to.

    If you really, truly think the book is worthy of your time and effort, I recommend that everyone who feels this way buy one extra copy for six months and pass 'em along to different friends, getting them hooked.

    I say six months for two reasons. One: it's going to take that long for the initial sales figures to reach Archie, and Two: it's going to take several months of steady sales to encourage the publishers to maintain KNUCKLES on a monthly basis. As it stands now, after issue #35, there's a very real chance of a "skip" month between every 3-issue story, resulting in only nine issues a year of KNUCKLES. Once that happens, it'll be a long time, if ever, before KNUCKLES is fully restored to 12 issues a year.

    If anyone has really looked at what's on the magazine racks these days, you know that both the retailer and the distributor make more money selling Time, Newsweek, Better Homes and Gardens, etc., than they do selling comic books. Therefore, if you wish to save that which you enjoy, you have to make the effort to show that support. The more fans that do come through, the louder the message will be.

    So start by spreading the word, and if anyone has any questions, don't hesitate to ask.

    The Ken went on to say that:

    One of the factors I didn't carefully explain is why a book like KNUCKLES can be on the endangered list, despite being one of the better selling titles on the market. Because Sega owns KNUCKLES, Archie has to pay them a fee each time an issue is published, therefore when you factor this in and other budget costs, it costs Archie more to produce KNUCKLES than, say, your typical issue of BETTY & VERONICA. Because of that, if Archie's share of the profit on KNUCKLES doesn't exceed that of BETTY & VERONICA's, the company doesn't consider publishing the book to be worthwhile to them. After all, they don't own the character, so they can't exploit it like they can their own.

    Personally, I don't understand it, either, but the concept of not making enough of a profit is just as bad as actually losing money to a lot of companies these days. They expect to make a certain amount, and if they don't make it, they look for any excuse to cut costs, figuring the product wasn't good enough even though other factors, such as distribution difficulties and costs in this case, were the main reason the product wasn't exposed to the widest possible audience looking for it.

    So what could we do to help? Personal suggestion: complain to Wal-mart, since their the ones that started this whole mess in the first place. When they get over 100 complaints on one thing, they'll probably question their decisions.

    SU Review - Vector 7:22pm CST, 4-5-99
    Allessandro Sanasi gave his opinions of the new cartoon on the newsgroup.

    Hello.

    About a week ago, Traitor (traitor@thegrid.net) asked me about my personal opinion about the show... for your reading pleasure, here is what I replied:

    First off, just in case some of you don't know already, I put up some nice online information about this cartoon at

    http://www.franken.de/users/deco/su/su.html
    and
    http://www.franken.de/users/deco/su/su_news.html

    It gives a general overview of the characters, as well as some basic information about the plot itself.

    IMHO, Sonic Underground can be considered as a mix between the SatAM cartoon and the syndicated "Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog" cartoon, with the stronger tendency going toward SatAM. The animation is well done, and the scenes show lots of dynamic and speed (especially during fights), sometimes even more dynamic than they had in SatAM.

    However, while SatAM showed Robotnik as someone who wanted to 'own' the whole planet, turn everyone with no exception into robots and pollute everything with his machines, forcing the Freedom Fighters to hide away in the forest, in the new cartoon he is only a 'normal' dictator who is satisfied to 'own' only _Robotropolis_ and rule over the rest of the population with his army of SWATbots, without roboticizing them. Therefore, most of the cities on Mobius are still undamaged and well populated. The aristocratic (rich) furries on Mobius even have some kind of VIP status because Robotnik needs their support (mostly money) to finance his dictatorship.

    Of course Robotnik still captures and roboticizes furries once in a while, but it's only a very small fraction of the total population. Also, the furries aren't turned totally into metal, they simply get robotic 'parts' added that make them obedient slaves.

    Another thing to mention is that the 'variety' of different-looking species that we had in the SatAM version is pretty much gone. Most of the supporting cast looks pretty generic. Usually they have human-shaped bodies (although a lot slimmer), 'dog' faces with button noses and small 'dog' ears. When they do use characters that differ from this generic style, usually they are hideously ugly and mis-shaped. The 'hip' Mobian outfit consisting only of gloves, shoes and maybe a belt or hat is also pretty much gone. Apart from Sonic & siblings, usually everyone is completely dressed.

    Sonic, Manic and Sonia all have different personalities, abilities and skills, which IMHO is a plus. For example, Sonia is the more adept person when it comes to things like remote-controlling a plane or scanning for SWATbots, and is also a very emotional and caring person, while Manic is better with opening locks, pickpocketing and bypassing security. Sonic is his usual fast, witty and impatient self as always, and the born leader and fighter.

    This variety among the three main characters _does_ offer a lot of possibilities for interesting plots, but when comparing it with SatAM I have to say that it 'stays behind'. Or to say it simply, I think it is more interesting when for example Sonic's girlfriend Sally gets captured by Robotnik than Sonic's sibling Sonia, or when Sally falls for a cute handsome guy and Sonic trying to re-gain her attention than when Sonia falls in love. It's a fact that people always care more when for example cute little Tails gets captured than when it happens to 'heroes' like Manic, who can pretty much fight back on their own.

    All the interesting relationships that the Knothole Freedom Fighters offered, like Sonic's and Sally's love for one another (although none of them wanted to admit it openly), Sally's relationship to Tails, who loved him almost like her own kid, while Tails called her 'Aunt', Tails' adoration for Sonic, Bunnie's cuteness and friendliness, although she suffered greatly from being half robot, Antoine the jerk, who always tried to impress the princess, or Rotor the tinkerer, always there with a fitting gadget when there was a need. But in the new cartoon, the main cast is only those three hedgehogs, and all relationship that will ever be among them, and all their reason to come to the rescue when one of them is in danger will always be nothing more but the relationship that is between siblings. Sonic coming to the rescue for his 'love', or to save his 'little bro', and all the fun interaction that was possible between the SatAM characters will not be possible in the new cartoon. Sonic & siblings still make jokes among each other, and still have conflicts and emotional stuff to handle, but because they are brothers and sister, it will never get as deep as it was possible in SatAM.

    And finally, because Sonic & siblings have formed a band in this series, each episode features a small musical interlude, either a 'stage performance' or something in the line of an 'MTV video clip' thrown in.

    Now to sum it all up, IMHO Sonic Underground is worth watching, especially when you already are a Sonic fan. It has pretty decent artwork, mostly interesting, funny and exciting plots, fast-paced action, and Sonic & siblings all act pretty cool, especially during fights. The stories are less 'dark' than SatAM was (mainly because Robotnik doesn't want to pollute the whole planet and turn all living beings into robots, but is instead satisfied to just rule over its population), but luckily don't slip to the AoStH level. The supporting characters are more 'generic' in appearance, taking away a bit from the true 'Mobian' feeling. What SatAM fans will miss the most however is the lack of characters like Sally or Tails... but I guess we have to live with that.

    As a final note, I would like to tell everyone that IMHO the musical interludes _suck_, because they almost always feel like a 'break' in the storyflow than helping it along, and all the added computer effects (to give it the typical 'MTV' feel) do the rest. ;)

    And that's that...

    Bye


    Alessandro
    ---
    You get what anyone gets. You get a lifetime.

    Good news (depending on your taste) and bad news, I guess. IMHO, it sounds like DiC has handled Robotnik better than they had in the previous cartoons, though the "generic furries" sound rather disappointing.
