Paul Castiglia To Leave Archie! - Zifei 3:57pm CST, 3-19-99
Paul Castiglia, the first writer, supporter of the Sonic comic, and the current Promotional Director at Archie, is going to leave Archie Comics to pursue a better job. Here's what he said:

Greetings, all. Paul Castiglia here. I apologize for the form letter, however, since I've made many friends and acquaintances here online, I thought it might be the best way to spread the news of my imminent departure from Archie Comics. Since January, 1990, I've been on staff at Archie, wearing several hats including Manager Of Direct Sales, Promotional Director, Archivist, New Product Development, occasional Editor and Archie Website content provider. I also did some freelance writing for them from time to time. I'm leaving to pursue a career at Arcotoys, which is a division of Mattel based in New York.

I will be a copywriter-- strike that-- THE copywriter for all their packaging, catalog, website, ads, etc. (with occasional copy farmed out to freelancers when the workload is overloaded). The benefits are tremendous, and I'll also have the opportunity to submit and develop new product ideas, which is an opportunity only open to Mattel employees. So I'm excited. At the same time, I'm a little melancholy because I've spent a great deal of time at Archie, learned so much and met so many people. But I do not intend to leave comics behind entirely. Archie has assured me I can continue to write stories for them on a freelance basis and I've also contacted a few other editors at other publishers. So perhaps my name will continue to appear in Archie publications in the future. My last day at Archie will be Friday, April 9th.

No word yet on who will replace him.

Elias' Age Question... - Zifei 3:40pm CST, 3-17-99
In Knuckles #24's Q&As, Sonic Editor Justin Freddy said that Elias was five years old when he crashed on the Floating Island, but he didn't look five in the comic...Ken Penders explains:

Here's the straight scoop. When working on THE FORBIDDEN ARC storyline, it was always my intent that Elias was older then Sally, even if they were twins and it was a matter of him emerging first. Upon discussing the Sally's brother when the storyline was first proposed, Justin automatically decided Elias was older, but at that point not sure by how much. (He also asked me if I had a name, and if not, since Karl would be handling the character much more than I he asked if Karl could contribute suggestions. Since I hadn't any specific name in mind, it was Karl who came up with Elias, which we all liked.)

During the production of KNUCKLES #19 through 21, Manny had decided he could no longer handle full-length stories on a regular basis because of his job at Nickelodeon, so some things got overlooked while we were revamping everything in order to keep the production schedule on track. One of those things was that Elias should have been depicted as a toddler instead of an infant when he left the Floating Island.

Regarding the letters page in question, Justin now admits he goofed in saying Elias was five when he should have said he was two or three at most.

Hope this clears it up for everyone...

Paul Castiglia Comments. - Zifei 8:32pm CST, 3-16-99
Paul Castiglia, manager of sales and promotion, replied to the harsh critiques about the Sonic Super Specials:

I'm writing concerning Sonic crossovers in general, and the Sonic/Sabrina crossover in particular. Here's the deal from where we sit at Archie: the 48-page specials serve a function. They allow us a venue wherein we can do the stories Sega wants us to do which have specific appeal to younger fans. This includes crossovers. Simply put, you should consider these stories like the DC Comics "Elseworlds" stories. In other words, they are primarily "What If?" stories that should not affect the regular continuity of the Sonic universe as portrayed in the monthly Sonic comic books.

We feel this effectively allows us to do stories that ARE continuity-based in the monthly Sonic comic. You might say the 48-page specials are a "necessary evil" that allow the monthly Sonic comic series to exist as its own entity, with its own continuity, and where more serious subject matter can be brought up from time to time. The bottom line: No [one] HAS to buy the 48-page specials-- no one is forcing you. You do so at your own will. If you think they're too babyish, simply save your money and just buy the monthly Sonic series. Leave the 48-page specials to the many kids who buy, read and enjoy them. Hope this makes sense to everyone...
