And Another SA Short Review... - Zifei 11:58am CST, 12-24-98
This game is sounding more and more fun...

At first, it seemd to be an "ok" game. the gameplay is great, but it took me a few mins to get used to it. there is some noticeable pop up on the game, sadly. its very noticeable in some areas. also, the camera sometimes doesnt position itself right, so you have to be messing with it manually. (R L shifts spins camera around, digital D pad is used to look around) the first two stages were good. I liked them, but was kind of not too impressed. then I got to the "wind" stage, and, was blown away (litteraly hehe). that stage had me on the edge of my bed, with all kinds of twists, spins, even playing upside down. there was a big tornado following me, wich took up the whole screen, and looked amazing.

Then I went back to the city (the stage you start in) and there were people walking down the streets, cars rolling up and down the streets, and it was dawn. the longer I played, the darker it got, untill it was night time. I stepped infront of a car, and it suddenly stopped, and more cars kept stopping until there was a traffic jam!

After seeing that, I know what to expect from the following stages. but I need to play longer. at least past the wind stage.

This review came from shadowblade. Traffic jam eh? I wonder if you could actually get hit by a car...or try to attack the people.

Problems In SA. - Zifei 9:32pm CST, 12-22-98
There are some problems with Sonic Adventure, which came out today in Japan and China. According to Next-Generation, the two main problems are:

There are two criticisms in which 3 of the 4 editors agree on. First, the game at times is so fast that it is difficult to see and play, causing unnecessary frustration on the gamer's part. Secondly, they point out that there are situations where the camera system is poorly utilized causing blind spots for the player to navigate characters through. Despite these minor shortcomings, the editors collectively agree that Sonic Adventure is the first AAA product for Dreamcast and that it shows off what Dreamcast is capable of doing.

Ha! Too fast! I like it! Now we know for sure that Sonic Adventure would promise us the experience of beinging Sonic himself, travelling at super-sonic speeds.
