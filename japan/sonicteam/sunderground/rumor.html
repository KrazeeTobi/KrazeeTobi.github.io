The Rumor that Started it All

As many of you already know, soon a new Sonic cartoon series called "Sonic Underground" will be aired. Sonic Underground is still in production by DiC Entertainment. Not much is know at the moment about the new cartoon, but some information has been released. Alas, SEGA still keeps most of it under wraps, but here's an overview of the info that has already leaked out... and more. Since everything is still unofficial, of course the final version may differ from what I 've heard. The main plot is that Sonic, his brother Manic, and his sister Sonia, form a band known as Sonic Underground. Together, they travel from town to town cheering up saddened Mobians. At the same time, they are searching for their mother, Queen Aleena Hedgehog, who is the ruler of Mobius. Sheriff Robotnik is out to marry Aleena so that he may become the king.


The first season of the series will have 40 episodes, with animation done by DiC France. Sonic's voice is again done by the all-famous Jaleel "Urkel" White, the same person who did it in previous Sonic series. The show is supposed to be a mix between the syndicated and the SatAM cartoon, although with a completely new setup. So far there seem to be NO references to the original SatAM cartoon. This means no Sally, no Bunnie, no Rotor, no Antoine, no Dulcy, not even Tails! Apart from Sonic and Robotnik, no other of the established SEGA characters seem to be in.

While indeed it is a rather interesting plot, many fans are upset at the fact that DiC didn't continue producing the former Saturday Morning Version of Sonic The Hedgehog. Many elements from Sonic are missing from Sonic Underground. For example, characters such as the Freedom Fighters are missing in action. Tails isn't even supposed to make an appearance. There is rumor that Knuckles might appear as a one-shot character, but know one knows.

The SatAM series already had an establised set of nicely-developed characters, a huge fan following, and even a comic book based on it. For years we all hoped for a renewal, but SEGA deliberately ignored all this and decided to start over. It would have been easy for them to incorporate the 'classic' continuity into the new one, but they didn't even try. On the other hand, what did we expect of a company like SEGA, which always supported only the crappy "Adventures of Sonic the Hedgehog" series instead of the superior SatAM?

Anyhow, more information will be posted here as it is released. 40 episodes are currently in the making. The show should be out by Spring '99, and is supposedly being released with a Bug! (another Sega Mascot) Cartoon.
